In vitro diagnostic medical devices (IVDs)

You must follow the conformity assessment procedure appropriate for the category of IVD concerned.

The categories of devices are explained in Part IV of the UK MDR 2002, Annex II List A and List B (as modified by Part III of Schedule 2A).

These categories are:

  • general IVDs not included in another list
  • IVDs for self-testing (a device intended by the manufacturer to be able to be used by lay persons in a home environment) excluding self-test devices included in other lists
  • IVDs in the List B classifications, including reagents products for rubella, toxoplasmosis and phenylketonuria, and devices for self-testing for blood sugar
  • IVDs in the List A classifications including reagents and products for HIV I and II, hepatitis B, C and D, and reagent products for determining ABO systems and anti-Kell including those used to test donated blood plus tests for screening vCJD

Conformity assessment routes are detailed in Regulation 40 of Part IV of the UK MDR 2002 (as modified by Part III of Schedule 2A).

You can find out more about conformity assessments for IVDs.
