Place a medical device: step by step

Find out how to place a medical device on the market in Great Britain.

  1. Step 1: Check you have a medical device according to the regulations

    If your device is defined as a medical device under the under the Devices Regulations 2002 (SI 2002 No 618, as amended), you must follow the process set out by the MHRA to ensure you comply with the legislation.

    1. Check if you have a medical device
    2. Medical devices and the regulations
    3. Medical devices and other health products

  2. Step 2: Find out which class of general medical device you have

    General medical devices are given a classification depending on the level of risk associated with them.

    1. Check the class of your medical device
    2. Classifying medical devices
    3. MEDDEV 2.4/1: classification of medical devices

  3. Step 3: Find out if you need to notify us about a clinical investigation

    You may need to tell the MHRA about your clinical investigation 60 days before it starts.

    1. Find out if you need to notify us about a clinical investigation
    2. Check if an exemption applies to your device
    3. How to notify the MHRA about your investigation

  4. Step 4: Arrange a conformity assessment

    Find out which conformity route you need to follow.

    1. Check which conformity assessment route you need to follow
    2. Complying with other designated standards
    3. Find a UK appproved body

  5. Step 5: Applying a UKCA marking

    You must put the UKCA marking on any medical devices placed on the UK market.

    1. Using the UKCA marking
    2. UKCA marking exemptions

  6. Step 6: Register your medical device

    Medical devices must be registered with the MHRA using the MORE platform.

    1. Register your medical device
    2. Help using MORE
    3. Registration fees

  7. Step 7: Manage your device after placing it on the market

    Once your medical device has been placed on the market you must continue to manage and monitor your device.

    1. Post-market surveillance
    2. Field safety notices and corrective actions
    3. Report an adverse incident
